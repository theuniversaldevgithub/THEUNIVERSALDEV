About Charlie Puth

Charlie Puth is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. He was born on December 2, 1991, in Rumson, New Jersey, USA. Puth gained international fame in 2015 with his hit song "See You Again," which he recorded with rapper Wiz Khalifa as a tribute to the late actor Paul Walker, who appeared in the "Fast and Furious" film series.
Puth has released several successful solo albums, including "Nine Track Mind" (2016) and "Voicenotes" (2018), which features hits like "One Call Away," "Attention," and "How Long." In addition to his solo work, Puth has collaborated with many other artists, including Meghan Trainor, Selena Gomez, and Lil Wayne. He is known for his soulful voice, pop and R&B-infused sound, and his ability to play multiple musical instruments.
